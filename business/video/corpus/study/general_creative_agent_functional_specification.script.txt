Next came the sparse constraint, and this is central. Now, the specification does put all of this into a formal mathematical equation, but the idea behind the math is what matters. A creativity score for any candidate idea is built by multiplying several factors together. There's a novelty or surprise term, capturing how improbable or far-flung the idea is. There's that balance function shaped like an inverted U, which rewards moderate surprise and penalizes both the obvious and the absurd. There's a usefulness term tuned to the domain. There's a coherence term, checking whether the pieces actually connect. And there's a feasibility term, asking whether you could really build it. Multiply them all, and you get a single creativity score. And the hard rule baked into the system is that sweet spot of one to four outlier dimensions. Too many and you get noise.
