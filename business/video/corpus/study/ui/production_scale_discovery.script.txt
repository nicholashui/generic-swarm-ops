So how does the system actually figure out the right scale? When a user describes their idea, or selects a showcase, the system runs a quick, inexpensive inference step. Think of it as a video production consultant. Given a description of what someone wants to create, it recommends the best production scale and template. It weighs the platform, since social means short, web means medium, and cinema means long. It considers the content type, where an ad is fifteen to sixty seconds, an explainer is one to three minutes, and a narrative runs three minutes or more. It factors in complexity, where a talking head is simple, visual effects are complex, and multiple scenes mean a longer piece. And it accounts for audience expectations and how sensitive the budget is. From all of that it returns a recommended template, a duration range, a low, mid, and high cost estimate, an estimated production time, the number of agents needed, an aspect ratio, its reasoning, and a set of alternatives, each with its own rationale and tradeoff.
